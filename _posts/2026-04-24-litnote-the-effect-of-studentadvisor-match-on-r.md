## 📌 Core Research Question

How should colleges assign students to academic advisors, and what matching characteristics between advisors and students most effectively improve retention and academic performance?

## 🔬 Methodology

**Identification Strategy:** Randomized Control Trial (RCT) with administrative data
- **Setting:** Small Dutch liberal arts college with random assignment of faculty advisors to students
- **Natural experiment design:** Exploits random advisor assignment to identify causal effects of different types of advisor-student matching
- **Matching dimensions analyzed:** Three similarity characteristics used as treatment variables:
  - Academic department (deep-level similarity)
  - Origin/background (experiential similarity) 
  - Gender (surface-level similarity)
- **Outcomes measured:** Student grades, academic standing, and exit/retention rates
- **Theoretical framework:** Similarity-attraction paradigm

## 📊 Key Findings

- **Academic department matching:** Positive causal effects on student outcomes (grades, academic standing, retention)
- **Origin/demographic matching:** No significant effects on student success measures
- **Gender matching:** No significant effects on student success measures
- **Key insight:** Disciplinary proximity matters more than shared demographic characteristics for advisor effectiveness
- **Policy implication:** Advisor assignment can serve as a low-cost intervention to improve student success
