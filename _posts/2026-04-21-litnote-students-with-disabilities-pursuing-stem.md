## 📌 Core Research Question

How do disparities in high school mathematics preparation between students with and without disabilities affect their progression through the STEM educational pipeline from high school to postsecondary degree completion?

The study employs **multinomial logistic regression** using data from the Education Longitudinal Study (ELS). The identification strategy appears to be a longitudinal analysis comparing outcomes between students with and without disabilities as they progress through the STEM pipeline. ## 📊 Key Findings
(Include specific numbers/effect sizes if available)

- 10th grade students with disabilities take significantly less advanced high school mathematics compared to students without disabilities
- This initial disparity in math preparation creates persistent gaps that continue through college STEM participation
- The mathematical preparation gap places students with disabilities at a systematic disadvantage in accessing STEM college opportunities, even when those opportunities might appear formally equivalent
- The cumulative effect suggests that apparent "equal access" to STEM programs masks underlying inequalities rooted in earlier educational stages
